Calculation Method

§ 1.2

For names — sum the letter values, then reduce. For dates — reduce day, month, and year separately, then sum and reduce again. The component method is the professional standard because it preserves Master Numbers at each stage.

i
For a name, write only the letters — remove spaces and punctuation. Assign each letter its value from the chart of the system you're using. Sum all values for the compound number, then reduce digit-by-digit for the root number.
ii
For a date, reduce the day, the month, and the year each to a single digit (or a Master Number, if that's what they reduce to). Then sum those three reduced values. Reduce the result the same way.
iii
Master Numbers 11, 22, 33 are not reduced further in the Pythagorean tradition. They mark amplified expressions of root 2, 4, and 6. Chaldean tradition does not formally recognize Master Numbers.
Worked example — name
N(5) + O(7) + V(6) + A(1) = 19  →  1+9 = 10  →  1+0 = 1
Chaldean — "Nova": Compound 19, Root 1. (Pythagorean would yield 16 → 7.)
Worked example — date
Day 27 → 2+7 = 9  ·  Month 11 = 11 (Master)  ·  Year 2026 → 2+0+2+6 = 10 → 1
Sum: 9 + 11 + 1 = 21 → 2+1 = 3
November 27, 2026: Date Number 3, with the Master vibration of 11 carried through the month.

A position, not consensus
Many practitioners use Chaldean for naming and Pythagorean for personal readings. Indian numerology schools often use Chaldean for everything; some Western practitioners use only Pythagorean. This guide follows the split because the reasoning for naming (phonetic vibration over alphabetical order) is sound — but it is one school's position, not universal doctrine.

Compound Number Meanings

§ 1.4

Chaldean compound numbers carry the primary interpretive weight. Names marked ★ are Cheiro's original labels. Numbers 34 and above have thinner classical sourcing — treat with proportionate caution.

A note on the planetary attributions
Classical Chaldean numerology assigns 4 to Rahu (North Node) and 7 to Ketu (South Node) — shadow points from Vedic tradition. The modern Western substitution of Uranus (4) and Neptune (7) is widely repeated but anachronistic — those planets were unknown to the Chaldeans. This guide shows both for transparency.

ii. Personal Numbers

Two numbers about you.

Your Life Path is your numerological identity — calculated once from your birth date. Your Personal Year is the energy of the calendar year you're inside right now. Together they form the personal context every decision sits within.

Life Path Number

§ 2.1

Calculated by reducing your birth day, birth month, and birth year separately, then summing. Master Numbers 11, 22, and 33 are detected at each stage and preserved.

Personal Year Number

§ 2.2

The energy of the current calendar year, derived from your birth day, birth month, and the present year. Some traditions run this birthday-to-birthday; this guide uses the calendar-year convention.
